## "Eavesdropping by Algorithm"

Babst Calland, a Pittsburgh-based law firm, published a briefing this month on the legal risks of AI meeting tools. Their phrasing cut through the usual PR language: AI meeting bots are, functionally, algorithmic eavesdropping systems.

Not surveillance. Not data collection. Eavesdropping.

The word choice matters. Eavesdropping has a specific legal meaning â€” listening to a conversation you're not a party to, without consent. When a bot joins your call on behalf of someone else, that's exactly what happens.

## The Architecture Is the Policy

Here's what most people miss: this isn't a settings problem. It's an architecture problem.

Meeting bots â€” whether from Otter, Fireflies, Fathom, Notion, or anyone else â€” share a fundamental design: audio leaves your device. It travels to company servers. It gets processed there. It gets stored there.

That architecture makes certain things impossible:
- You can't guarantee who has access to the recording
- You can't prevent it from being used to train models
- You can't protect it from a breach you didn't cause
- You can't stop it from appearing in a legal discovery process
